How can you treat and help those who are mentally disabled?

Treatment of mental disorders take a lot of time which includes diagnosis, classifying your disorder, and then treatement.
A specific team can be assigned for you which includes:-

  • Primary Care Doctor
  • Nurse
  • Psychiatrist
  • Psychotherapist(or a licensed counselor)
  • Pharmacists
  • Your close ones(Family, relatives, friends, colleagues, etc.)

  • Medications

    Although psychiatric medications don't cure mental illnesses, they can often significantly improve symptoms. Psychiatric medications can also help make other treatments, such as psychotherapy, more effective. Some medications include:-
    1. Anti-depressannts
    2. Anti-anxiety pills
    3. Mood-Stabilizing pills
    4. Anti-psychotic pills

  • Psychotherapy

    • Psychotherapy, also called talk therapy, involves talking about your condition and related issues with a mental health professional.
      Talk therapies are very productive because a person starts to learn more about himself, his feeling, emotions, etc. This facilitates stress management skills.
    • Psychotherapy often can be successfully completed in a few months, but in some cases, long-term treatment may be needed. It can take place one-on-one, in a group or with family members.
    • When you choose a therapist, you should be sure that he/she can listen to how you live your life, understand your life journey, and provide some good advice.

  • Participating in your own care

    Working together, you and your primary care provider or mental health professional can decide which treatment may be best, depending on your symptoms and their severity, your personal preferences, medication side effects, and other factors.
